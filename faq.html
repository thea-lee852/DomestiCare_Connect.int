Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions ▲

Residency and Work Location

You are required to reside at your employer’s residence as specified in the contract. You should work only at the designated address mentioned in the contract, except for incidental duties performed outside, such as grocery shopping or car washing.

Domestic Duties

You are expected to carry out the duties outlined in the Standard Employment Contract, which include household chores, cooking, child-minding, and elderly care.

Restriction on Outside Employment

You must not engage in any other form of employment or work for other employers.

Early Termination

If you wish to terminate the contract early, you must provide one month’s notice or agree to payment in lieu of notice, unless justified by circumstances such as fear of violence or disease, ill-treatment by the employer, or permanent medical unfitness after five or more years of employment.

Legal Consequences of Breach

Breaching the contract or the conditions of your stay may result in prosecution, deportation, or a ban on future employment in Hong Kong.

Specific Questions ▼

Eligibility

Q: Who can hire a foreign domestic helper in Hong Kong?
A: Employers must be Hong Kong residents with a household income of at least HK$15,000 per month or equivalent financial assets.

Q: Can I hire multiple helpers?
A: Yes, but you must demonstrate sufficient financial means to support multiple helpers.

Employment Contract

Q: What is the Standard Employment Contract?
A: It is a government-prescribed contract (ID 407) that sets out the terms of employment, including wages, duties, living arrangements, and other obligations.

Q: Can I modify the terms in the Standard Employment Contract?
A: No, all terms are standardized and must comply with Hong Kong laws.

Working Conditions

Q: Can the helper live outside my residence?
A: No, the helper must reside at your residence as specified in Clause 3 of the contract.

Q: Can I require my helper to perform duties outside domestic work?
A: No, helpers can only perform domestic duties as specified, such as household chores, cooking, child-minding, and elderly care.

Q: Can my helper follow my family overseas?
A: Yes, with mutual consent. However, ensure the helper has proper travel documents and insurance coverage.

Wages and Allowances

Q: What is the Minimum Allowable Wage (MAW) for helpers?
A: As of September 29, 2023, the MAW is HK$4,870 per month.

Q: Do I need to provide food?
A: Yes, you must either provide food free of charge or pay a food allowance of at least HK$1,236 per month.

Rest Days and Holidays

Q: How many rest days must I provide?
A: At least one rest day per week.

Q: Are helpers entitled to statutory holidays?
A: Yes, after working for three months, helpers are entitled to paid statutory holidays.

Termination of Employment

Q: Can I terminate the contract early?
A: Yes, by giving one month’s notice or paying one month’s wages in lieu of notice.

Q: Can I dismiss a helper without notice?
A: Yes, in cases of serious misconduct, such as fraud or habitual neglect of duties.

Q: What should I do if the contract is terminated?
A: Notify the Immigration Department and Labour Department within seven days of the termination.

Insurance and Medical Care

Q: Do I need to provide insurance for the helper?
A: Yes, you must take out an insurance policy under the Employees' Compensation Ordinance to cover work-related injuries.

Q: Am I responsible for the helper’s medical expenses?
A: Yes, you must cover all medical fees and expenses incurred during the helper’s employment.

Legal Restrictions

Q: Can my helper work for someone else or take up a side job?
A: No, helpers are prohibited from working for other employers or taking non-domestic jobs.

Q: What are the penalties for breaching employment laws?
A: Employers may face fines or imprisonment for violations, such as failing to provide insurance or engaging helpers in unauthorized work.

Renewal and Visa Applications

Q: How do I renew my helper’s contract?
A: Submit a new Standard Employment Contract and visa extension application to the Immigration Department before the current visa expires.

Q: How long does the visa application process take?
A: Visa applications typically take 4-6 weeks to process.

Other Issues

Q: What should I do if disputes arise?
A: Contact the Labour Department for mediation or file a claim with the Labour Tribunal.

Q: Are live-in helpers subject to the Statutory Minimum Wage (SMW)?
A: No, live-in foreign domestic helpers are exempt from SMW but must receive the Minimum Allowable Wage.
